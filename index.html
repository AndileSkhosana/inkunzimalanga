Company Background

Inkunzimalanga Holdings was established in 2015 by Sipho Mnguni.

Inkunzimalanga aims to work very closely with experts with over 30 years of industry experience thus making the company very flexible and diverse to ensure service beyond excellence as & when presented with an opportunity.

Inkunzimalanga Holdings aspire to offer business to the energy, mining, construction, manufacturing and government sectors as the company specializes in Civil construction, Plant hire, Transportation & Logistics, Electrical & Mechanical Engineering, Mining & Rehabilitation, Industrial cleaning and Mining supplies.

Company Philosophy

Inkunzimalanga corporate culture is characterized by its ability to adapt quickly to clients' needs and requirements. This is done by bringing a sharper focus to the requirements of the client and striving to leverage cutting-edge technology to provide the highest levels of service through cost-efficient methods.

We also pride ourselves with a culture and tradition of ingenuity, which has driven continuous improvement, and the relentless drive for innovation in the way we operate. Our focus is to challenge the status quo, working proactively with key stakeholders to find the best solutions and applications to assure sustainable, long-term relationships.

We focus on providing a variety of solutions for the clients to choose from because of not subscribing to a specific brand, we are able to provide alternative options (Premium to unpopular but good brands) that have similar or even superior capabilities in terms of performance.

Safety and Quality Standards

INKUNZIMALANGA Holdings maintains a strict safety policy in all mining and industrial operations, ensuring full compliance with regulations and prioritizing the protection of employees and stakeholders.

Strict Safety Protocols

  • Mandatory PPE (Personal Protective Equipment):
    • All employees and contractors must wear appropriate PPE (helmets, gloves, boots, reflective clothing, respirators, etc.) before entering any worksite.
  • Daily Safety Inspections: Supervisors must conduct routine inspections of work areas before shifts begin to identify and address potential hazards.
